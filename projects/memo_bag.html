works | social, materials, product | memo bag

Please note that this is still an ongoing research project at the time of writing in Mar 2025. Specific design decisions and details are omitted, feel free to reach out to me (contact page!) to learn more about the project.


Benjamin Phua is a PhD candidate in the Monash Creative Technologies Lab. His interests lie in the applications of machine learning, deep learning and data science to health-related matters, especially as they relate to marginalised groups. Ben's current research project investigates technologies to reduce the loss of personal items by neuro-divergent university students who regularly lose things.


Memo Bag is one of the many technologies created as a portable solution for when people are out and about. With our audience at heart, the design explores making methods and materials that are low-effort and economical. The current refinement of the Memo Bag integrates techniques from modularity, transformable fashion and visual inventory management.
